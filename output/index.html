Rules

  1. Everyone may only submit 1 entry. If the form is submitted multiple times, the last submitted entry will be judged.

  2. You may use anything you designed, if it is for the competition or an earlier MOC of yours.

  3. You may not steal any designs and submit them as your own.

  4. The MOC must be in the Technic theme

Objective judging

These are clearly-set rules, which are not open for subjective opinions, but only objective facts.

  1. Part Count (relative to other submissions) (up to 10 points)
  2. Modification (exactly 5 points)

If your build is an Alternate Model, you can get these points:

  1. % of main model used (up to 5 points)

so

  1. Electronics (2 point)
  2. play-features (e.g. working steering, other mechanisms) (up to 5 points)
  3. Pneumatics (5 points)
  4. mimics real-life object (e.g. car replica) (1 point)

Subjective judging

These are very subjective categories, so these will be judged 75% by an open community-voting and 25% by me.

  1. overall aesthetic (up to 20 points)
  2. overall play-features (up to 10 points)
  3. overall display factor (up to 20 points)
  4. overall likelihood of building this MOC (up to 20 points)
  5. overall instructions quality (up to 10 points)
  6. overall image quality (up to 10 points)
  7. overall community favorite (100% by the community-voting) (up to 40 points)
  8. overall functionality / features (75% by me, 25% by community-voting) (up to 10 points)
  9. complexity of pneumatic functions (75% by me, 25% by community-voting) (up to 10 points)

If your model represents real-life objects (e.g. car replicas), you can get these points: 1. accuracy (up to 5 points)

This comes to a maximum score of 188 points! However it is very unlikely that anyone gets all points

All points for each category will be published, so you can review where you got how many points and understand your result.
